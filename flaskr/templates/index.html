Indexing a space filling curve

This page visualizes the functions I wrote (described here) using Plotly to plot the position of a point on a (pseudo) space filling curve, given the orthant sequence (translating from alpha space to numeric space).

Basically, given a sequence of the letters a, b, c, d (each letter corresponds with a quadrant on the x,y plane), this function plots that point in x,y coordinates.

One of the beauties of the Hilbert curve is that it preserves locality (i.e. a point at a higher "level" (ex. dcab) is very close to the point at decreasing levels (i.e. dca)).

Instructions: To visulaize this, move the slider below to see how the sequence point corresponds at different levels of depth.

Instructions: Input below your own sequence containing a combination of the quadrants a,b,c,d to generate a new point on the hilbert curve. You may not input other letters.

Note: The quantity of letters in your sequence corresponds to the levels of "depth" in the curve. Ex. the sequence "a" corresponds to a curve level 1, the sequence "dcab" corresponds to a curve level 4, etc.

The longer your sequence, the longer it takes to generate. Please be patient & limit your sequence to a maximum length of 8. Thanks!
